# Difference Between NEFT, IMPS, and UPI

## What each method is
- UPI: instant mobile-based payments using UPI ID, QR code, or linked bank account
- IMPS: instant bank transfer using account number + IFSC or mobile banking
- NEFT: batch-processed bank transfer used for account-to-account movement

## When to use UPI
- Small to medium daily payments
- Merchant QR payments
- Quick peer-to-peer transfers

## When to use IMPS
- Urgent bank transfer when UPI is failing
- Higher-value transfer to a known bank account
- Cases where VPA or QR is unavailable

## When to use NEFT
- Planned business transfers to supplier bank accounts
- Large payments that do not need instant confirmation
- Situations where bank branch or net banking workflows are preferred

## Practical advice for merchants
If UPI repeatedly fails for a large business payment, switch to IMPS or NEFT instead of retrying many times and risking confusion.
